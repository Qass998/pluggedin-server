## THE 3 REAL PROBLEMS CONSTRUCTION OPERATORS HAVE

1. TENDER PIPELINE — Missing contracts and quoting jobs blind.
   No system to track what's been quoted, what's outstanding,
   what needs following up, or where the next job is coming from.

2. SUBCONTRACTOR COMPLIANCE — Expired public liability,
   lapsed CSCS cards, outdated CHAS/Constructionline accreditation.
   One site visit from HSE = shutdown.

3. MATERIALS INTELLIGENCE — Quoting on Thursday with prices
   that changed by Monday. Margin erosion no one sees coming.

Lead with tender pipeline — that's where the money is.

## TYPICAL ICP

- Established firm: 5-50 staff or subcontractors
- Turnover £500k-£5m
- Mix of domestic and commercial projects
- Owner-managed or ops director at the helm
- Uses spreadsheets and WhatsApp for project management
- Quotes done manually in Word/Excel
- Subcontractor compliance: stored in a filing cabinet
- Tender pipeline: ad hoc and reactive

Decision maker: Owner, MD, or commercial manager
Pain: "We're always chasing our tail. Good months and terrible months."
Real problem: No pipeline visibility. Reactive not proactive.

## SUBCONTRACTOR COMPLIANCE PITCH

"You are personally liable if an uninsured subcontractor
causes damage or injury on your site. You know that.

What's your current system for checking?
Spreadsheet? Filing cabinet? Their word?

The Compliance Agent holds every subcontractor's:
- Public liability insurance (with expiry)
- CSCS cards (per person)
- Constructionline / CHAS / SMAS status

30 days before anything expires: you get an alert.
They get a WhatsApp asking for the updated document.
If they don't provide it, they don't get the next job."
