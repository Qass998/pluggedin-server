## THE 3 REAL PROBLEMS SOLICITORS HAVE

1. MISSED ENQUIRIES — Calls and emails out of hours go unanswered.
   The prospect googles the next firm. Revenue lost, never seen.

2. SLOW FOLLOW-UP — Enquiries sit in inboxes. Partners are in court.
   The prospect got a callback from the firm that responded in 4 minutes.

3. COMPLIANCE DRIFT — Deadlines, certificate renewals, client milestones
   managed manually in spreadsheets or diaries. One mistake = SRA.

Lead with whichever of these lands first. Usually the first two together.

## TYPICAL ICP

- Boutique firm: 2-10 fee earners
- Specialises in 1-3 practice areas
- Gets 20-80 enquiries per month
- Converts 30-50% of qualified enquiries
- Average case value: £500 (conveyancing) to £5,000+ (PI, family)
- Managing partner checks email at 9pm and feels stressed
- No CRM — uses Excel or Outlook tasks for case tracking

Decision maker: Managing partner or practice manager
Pain: "We're busy but we're leaving money on the table"
Real problem: Enquiries falling through at every stage

---

## ICP SCORE MULTIPLIERS

+15 pts: Has high-value practice area (PI, clinical negligence, commercial)
+10 pts: Does not have a receptionist or uses an answering service
+10 pts: Growing firm (recent hires or new office)
+10 pts: Has Google reviews with enquiry intent ("easy to talk to")
+5 pts: Runs a lead gen campaign but doesn't track conversion
+5 pts: Managing partner is owner, not corporate structure

-10 pts: Large firm with internal marketing team
-10 pts: Legal aid only (low fees, high volume, wrong fit)
-5 pts: Locked in contract with another provider
