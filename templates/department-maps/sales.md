## THE SALES WORKFLOW (step by step)

Map exactly how a lead moves from first contact to signed client.
Be specific — this becomes the agent's operating procedure.

Step 1 — HOW A LEAD ENTERS THE SYSTEM:
What happens the moment a new lead comes in?
Who receives it? How? (phone / email / form / WhatsApp?)
What's done with it immediately?
How long does that typically take?
What's the average response time today?

Step 2 — LEAD QUALIFICATION:
Who qualifies the lead?
What questions are asked to qualify?
What makes a lead qualified vs unqualified?
Where is the qualification noted?

Step 3 — FIRST CONTACT / MEETING:
Who makes the first contact?
Call or meeting? In-person or virtual?
What's the standard pitch or presentation?
What materials are used (brochure, deck, proposal)?
How long after initial contact does this happen?

Step 4 — PROPOSAL / QUOTE:
Who creates it?
What tool? (Word / Excel / template / CRM?)
How long does it take to produce?
What's the typical follow-up process after sending?
How long before they chase if no response?

Step 5 — CLOSE:
What's the close rate on proposals sent?
What objections come up most often?
Who handles objections?
How is a deal confirmed? (signed contract / verbal / email?)
What triggers the handover to Operations/Delivery?

Step 6 — HANDOVER:
Who does Sales hand the client to?
What information is passed over?
How? (CRM handover / email / verbal briefing?)
What typically gets lost in this handover?

## AGENT CONFIGURATION NOTES

Based on this map, agents will handle:

AUTOMATED (no approval needed):
→ Lead capture from website / form / WhatsApp
→ Immediate acknowledgment to every new lead
→ CRM logging (Airtable)
→ Follow-up sequences if no response after 48hrs
→ Qualification scoring (based on criteria above)
→ Pipeline stage updates as deals progress

REQUIRES APPROVAL:
→ Sending a formal proposal
→ Applying a discount
→ Writing off a lost lead

→ New leads vs target
→ Qualified leads in pipeline
→ Proposals outstanding + days since sent
→ Close rate this month vs last month
